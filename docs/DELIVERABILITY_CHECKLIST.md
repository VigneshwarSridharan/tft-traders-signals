# Deliverability Spot-Check Checklist

The platform sends through existing Zoho Mail accounts (via SMTP app
passwords), so authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC) is inherited from Zoho's
domain configuration, not generated by this app. This checklist is about
*verifying* that inheritance holds and catching regressions before they tank
inbox placement — see [PRD §2.4](./PRD.md#24-tracking-pixel--click-tracking-architecture)
and the "Zero deliverability regression" success metric.

Run the one-time setup checks before go-live, and the periodic spot-check
routine on an ongoing basis (weekly is reasonable; more often right after
sending volume increases or after adding a new sender account/domain).

## One-time setup (per sending domain)

- [ ] **SPF**: `dig TXT yourdomain.com` includes `include:zoho.com` (or your
      region's Zoho SPF include, e.g. `zoho.eu`) and the record resolves to
      10 or fewer DNS lookups total (SPF's hard limit — exceeding it makes
      the whole record `permerror`, which fails auth for *everything*, not
      just the newest include).
- [ ] **DKIM**: Zoho Mail Admin Console → Mail settings → DKIM shows the
      selector as "Verified" for each sending domain in use. Confirm with
      `dig TXT <selector>._domainkey.yourdomain.com`.
- [ ] **DMARC**: `dig TXT _dmarc.yourdomain.com` exists, with `p=` at least
      `quarantine` (not `none`) once SPF/DKIM are confirmed passing, and an
      `rua=` reporting address you actually monitor.
- [ ] **Tracking domain**: `TRACKING_DOMAIN`/`TRACKING_HOST` is a subdomain
      of the *sending* domain (not a third-party domain) — see
      [README § Tracking domain](../README.md#tracking-domain-dns--tls).
      Third-party tracking domains are one of the more common causes of
      otherwise-authenticated mail landing in spam.
- [ ] **List-Unsubscribe**: send a test email and confirm both the
      `List-Unsubscribe` and `List-Unsubscribe-Post` headers are present
      (Task 21) — Gmail/Yahoo require one-click unsubscribe support for bulk
      senders as of their 2024 sender requirements.
- [ ] **Reverse DNS / sending IP reputation**: this is on Zoho's
      infrastructure, not ours — nothing to configure here, but note it as
      the thing to escalate to Zoho support if reputation-related bounces
      show up in the spot checks below and everything on our side checks
      out.

## Ongoing spot-check routine

Maintain a small seed list across the providers your customers actually use
— at minimum Gmail, Outlook/Microsoft 365, and Yahoo; add others your
customer base skews toward. Use real (or realistic test) mailboxes you
control at each provider, not disposable/temp-mail addresses — spam
filtering behaves differently for those.

For each spot check:

1. Compose and send a real (or representative test) email through the
   platform to every seed-list address.
2. Record, per provider:
   - [ ] Landed in **Inbox** / **Spam** / **Promotions or other tab** /
         **not delivered at all**
   - [ ] Time to delivery (should be seconds to low minutes; a large delay
         can itself be a reputation signal worth tracking over time)
   - [ ] Any visible sender warning banner ("this sender is unverified", etc.)
3. If anything lands outside Inbox, before assuming a global problem, rule
   out the boring causes first:
   - Was this sender account's IMAP/SMTP verification green in the
     dashboard (Sender Accounts page) at send time?
   - Did the template trip `sanitize-html` stripping, break the merge-field
     preview, or otherwise render with broken/garbled HTML? Bulk filters
     penalize malformed HTML.
   - Any recent bounce-rate or complaint-rate spike in the Analytics
     dashboard for this sender account? (Sustained >2-3% hard-bounce or any
     measurable spam-complaint rate is what triggers most providers'
     reputation throttling.)
4. Log the result (date, provider, placement) somewhere durable — even a
   simple running table in this file is enough to spot a trend before it
   becomes an outage:

| Date | Sender account | Gmail | Outlook | Yahoo | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| _(none yet — run the first spot check before go-live)_ | | | | | |

## Sender warm-up (new sending domain or long-idle account)

A brand-new sending domain/IP (or a sender account that's been idle for
weeks) has no reputation yet — sending full volume on day one is a common
way to get provider-throttled immediately. Ramp up instead:

- [ ] Days 1-3: low volume (tens of emails/day) to the most-engaged,
      most-likely-to-open customers first (recent repliers, high engagement
      score on the Customers page).
- [ ] Days 4-7: increase gradually (roughly double every 1-2 days) while
      watching bounce/complaint rate in Analytics — pause the ramp if either
      spikes.
- [ ] Week 2+: approach normal sending volume once a week of clean sending
      history (low bounces, opens/clicks in the expected range, no spot-check
      placement failures) has accumulated.

This is manual today — there's no automated warm-up scheduler in the
platform (see the "Suggested v2 backlog" in [TASKS.md](./TASKS.md) for
where an automated version would fit as a future feature).
